🎓 Introducing ACCMaR 🎓

ACCMaR is committed to combining state-of-the-art machine-learning techniques with advanced electronic structure methods for efficient simulations of chemistry and materials systems. Computation wise, we utilize quantum mechanical softwares (e.g., VASP, Quantum Espresso, CP2K, etc) and machine-learned force fields to investigate the atomic and electronic structures, from which we will interpret physical and chemical properties for the systems of interest. Methodology wise, (1) we seek to develop post-DFT methods with a particular focus on excited-state processes, such as charged and optical excitations, at materials interfaces; (2) we look forward to decoding intriguing chemical properties for functional materials from literature data, in which we will build AI Agent to facilitate data labeling and processing. 

We are currently recruiting researchers of all ranks with a background in one of the following: physics, chemistry, materials science, and computer science. Candidates that are interested in joining us are advised to refer to our news page for further information.
